# Anti-Patterns — 02_patterns-and-insights/anti-patterns/

What the evidence says NOT to do.

---

## A1 — Don't market on "organize your inventory / all in one place."
**Evidence:** E-001, E-023, E-024
You lose to Artwork Archive: 4.9★, 106 reviews, $6/mo, and it *owns* that exact claim. Marketing on consolidation walks straight into the incumbent's strongest, most-defended position. Inventory is a supporting feature, never the headline.

---

## A2 — Don't target galleries, institutions, foundations, or residencies.
**Evidence:** E-016, E-019, plus E-026/E-027 (segment data)
Galleries are entrenched with Artlogic (~6,000 of them; Artlogic is even embedded in the UBS report's gallery survey), and that segment is the squeezed/shrinking middle. Chasing institutions means fighting a deep incumbent for a contracting market. This is direct evidence *for* the ICP narrowing you already made — don't drift back.

---

## A3 — Don't chase AR / "view in a room" visualization features.
**Evidence:** E-021, E-022
ArtPlacer (and Canvy, ArtFlows) own visualization; it's adjacent, not core, and it carries its own trust complaints. Building AR pulls focus from the transaction backbone that is actually your moat. If visualization matters, integrate — don't rebuild.

---

## A4 — Don't add gallery-grade complexity to win "completeness."
**Evidence:** E-020, E-016
"Replicate gallery bureaucracy" is the incumbent's failure mode for independent artists. Complexity that impresses an enterprise buyer kills your solo ICP. Every feature must pass the test: does it make a solo artist's first week simpler or harder? Guard onboarding simplicity like a moat.

---

## A5 — Don't run an opaque or trap-style trial/billing.
**Evidence:** E-015, E-022
The category is full of refund refusals, surprise charges, and "misleading offer" complaints. A card-required trial is fine *only* if day-30 behavior is stated plainly and cancellation is one click. Any ambiguity here squanders a differentiator the whole category is failing at — and would make Artelier look like the thing it's positioned against.

---

## A6 — Don't write artist-voice copy where investor-voice is required (and vice versa).
**Evidence:** Pattern P2/P3 tension + known recurring failure mode
The buyer wants identity + empowerment + "what do I charge." The investor wants economics + moat + segment math. The evidence supports *both* audiences — but with different framings of the same facts (e.g., "price like a pro" for artists vs. "deterministic pricing engine = defensible moat" for investors). Conflating them is the recurring copy failure. Keep the two registers strictly separate.
