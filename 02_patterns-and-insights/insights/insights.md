# Insight Candidates — 02_patterns-and-insights/insights/

Five strategic takeaways, each attributed to the patterns behind it.

---

## I1 — Lead every funnel with PRICING. Inventory is a red ocean; pricing is open water.
**From:** P3, P6
Inventory/organization is owned by Artwork Archive at $6/mo with a 4.9★ moat. Pricing is the documented #1 pain in the category and the one thing no incumbent solves deterministically. Every headline, ad, and landing-page hero should answer the question artists are *already asking*: "what should I charge?" — and answer it with "math you can defend," not "AI magic." The internal line "AI to do the typing, math to do the pricing" is the public line, near-verbatim.

---

## I2 — Position as the anti-extraction, founder-owned, own-your-data alternative.
**From:** P4, plus E-008, E-017
The leaders carry an extraction stain: PE money, surprise price hikes, billing traps. Artelier's founder ownership + locked 15% community fund + "own your data, own your career" (vs. offloading your record to a gallery) is a values-based wedge that the *evidence* — not just conviction — supports. This is where the give-back stops being a footnote and becomes a reason to choose Artelier. It also sharpens the open société-à-mission question: binding the give-back makes the claim defensible to both investors and buyers.

---

## I3 — Bet the raise on the segment trend, not the $59.6B topline.
**From:** P5, P7
The honest, differentiated investor story isn't "the art market is huge." It's: *the only growing segment is the low/solo end (double-digit growth, sub-$500k), the middle is collapsing (−29% since 2010), transaction volume is decoupling from value and rising (40.5M, +3%), and incumbents are abandoning exactly the segment we serve.* Per-seat SaaS on rising transaction volume is insulated from art-price swings. That's a "why now" with teeth.

---

## I4 — Don't out-feature. Out-workflow.
**From:** P4 (E-016), P2
The leader is "built by developers, not gallerists." Artelier's 7-stage inquiry→one-sheet→invoice→sell→provenance→ship state machine is the differentiator *because it mirrors how art actually changes hands.* Marketing should show the workflow as a sequence the buyer recognizes from their own life, not a feature grid. The product's job is to feel like it was built by someone who has actually sold a painting in a DM — which is true.

---

## I5 — The trial must deliver value in days, which makes the calculator + upload bugs an existential marketing problem.
**From:** P1, plus E-013, E-014
Users tolerate price doubt only when locked in; they churn on stagnation; switchers arrive at a breaking point with low patience. Artelier's 30-day card-required trial only converts if week one produces a "holy shit, that's done" moment. A broken calculator or a silent bulk-upload failure during the trial doesn't just annoy — it *confirms the buyer's fear that switching is more pain than the spreadsheet.* The bug fixes are therefore upstream of the entire funnel. No public asset promising effortless onboarding can ship until they're true.

## SECOND PASS additions

## I6 — In France, don't teach a new method — automate the one the market already trusts.
**From:** P9
"Calcul au point" and "cote d'artiste" are already the accepted, gallery-taught way French artists price. Artelier's engine *is* this method, systematized. French copy and UI should use these exact terms, not a translated generic pitch. Localization here is a moat amplifier, not a translation job — it turns "learn our new tool" into "finally, the method you already use, done for you."

## I7 — The French e-invoicing mandate is the launch wedge AND a hard architecture decision.
**From:** P10
Treat 09/2026 as a marketing wedge ("be ready without thinking about it") only if the product is actually compliant. Emitting a PDF is non-compliant for French B2B by 09/2027. So the *real* next decision is whether Artelier integrates with / hands off to a Plateforme Agréée (PDP). Put the PDP question into the pending expert-comptable/avocat consult now — it gates whether the French invoicing story is true.

## I8 — For the manager ICP, lead with settlement, match roster table-stakes, win on pricing + ownership.
**From:** P8 (+ pass-1 P4)
The manager headline is "pay every artist correctly, automatically." Per-seat access and per-artist consignment are table stakes (ArtCloud already has them) — build to match, don't market as novel. Differentiate where incumbents can't follow: the deterministic pricing engine and the founder-owned, 15%-back, anti-extraction story (sharper now that the closest manager competitor sits inside the Artlogic PE roll-up).
