## Artelier, in one paragraph

> Artelier Business is a business operating system for living, primary-market artists and the people who manage them. It replaces the spreadsheet-plus-memory-plus-DMs workflow with one app that runs the whole job — from a defensible, deterministic price to inquiry, one-sheet, invoice, sale, provenance, and shipping — so independent artists and small managers can sell like professionals without gallery-grade cost or complexity.
> _Grounded in: research_brief.md (definition, defensible core, 7-stage workflow); T1, T2, T5._

## Defensible core

A **deterministic, rules-based pricing engine** — _"AI to do the typing, math to do the pricing."_ Pricing is the loudest unmet need in the category (E-009) and the one lane no incumbent occupies (E-023; P3). The engine's output is not just a number but a **defensible justification** an artist can explain to any collector (E-011). _(research_brief.md; Insight I1)_

## Who it's for

A single ICP spectrum on a per-artist-under-management seat:
1. **Solo artist (roster of one)** — sells directly, often via social/DM, anxious about pricing.
2. **Small artist-manager (2–15 artists)** — the founder's own position; needs per-artist separation and a sale workflow.
3. **Growing professional manager (15+ artists)** — the upper end of the same seat metric.

**Explicitly not for:** galleries, curators, universities, foundations, residencies, institutions. This narrowing is validated by competitor behavior and market structure, not just asserted. _(research_brief.md; T5; P5)_

**The manager wedge is roster settlement.** What distinguishes the manager from the solo artist is the need to pay a roster correctly — per-artist consignment splits, auto-calculated payouts, per-seat access. Mainstream tools ignore this (managers cobble consignment-shop software); the per-artist Settlement waterfall is Artelier's native answer: _"pay every artist correctly, automatically."_ Per-seat access is table stakes to match, not a differentiator. _(E-031, E-033, E-034; T8; Insight I8)_

## Operating principles

- **Pricing first.** Inventory is a red ocean; pricing is open water. We lead with "what should I charge?" and answer it with math you can defend, not AI magic. _(I1; T2)_
- **Defensible over arbitrary.** Every price ships with its rationale (size, tier, comp logic). The "why" is the feature manual pricing structurally cannot match. _(E-010, E-011)_
- **Out-workflow, don't out-feature.** We mirror how art actually changes hands; we resist creep toward gallery-grade bureaucracy. "Powerful but not bureaucratic." _(I4; E-016, E-020)_
- **Identity before utility.** We make the buyer feel like a professional first; "price like a pro," not "calculate faster." _(P2; E-002)_
- **Anti-extraction by default.** Transparent billing, one-click cancel, founder ownership, 15% community fund, own-your-data. We earn trust where incumbents lose it. _(P4; I2; E-015, E-017, E-008)_
- **The trial is the pitch.** Nothing promising effortless onboarding ships until it's true. Bug-fixes upstream of the funnel. _(I5; E-013, E-014)_
- **Evidence or it didn't happen.** No claim ships without provenance; unrecorded conversation is not evidence. _(decision-rights.md; internal-proof/evidence_log.md)_
- **In France, automate the method the market already trusts.** Speak "calcul au point" and "cote d'artiste" natively; the engine is that method systematized, not a new one to teach. Position it as a defensible floor, not a cage. _(E-035, E-036, E-037; I6; A8)_
- **Compliance claims must be true before they ship.** The French e-invoicing mandate (receive 09/2026, emit 09/2027; PDFs disqualified) is a real tailwind — but only if Artelier emits compliant invoices through an approved platform. We don't promise "compliant French invoicing" while emitting PDFs. _(E-038, E-039; I7; A7)_
