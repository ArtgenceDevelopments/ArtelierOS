## The market truths we operate against

**T1 — The real competitor is the spreadsheet, not other software. The switch trigger is the moment it breaks under volume.**
Across incumbent reviews and raw seller forums the default tool is a spreadsheet, and its failure mode is always the same: manual updating, no auto-refresh, drowning under load. Buyers don't decide to "buy software" — they hit a breaking point.
_Proof: E-006 ("spreadsheets quickly become cumbersome"), E-007 ("my spreadsheet just doesn't update itself"), E-028 (40.5M transactions, +3% volume). Pattern P1._

**T2 — Pricing is the loudest unmet need in the category and its single open lane.**
"How to price my original art" is the most-asked question among artists; the prevailing belief is fatalistic ("art is worth what someone will pay"); artists want a price they can _defend_. The dominant incumbent is silent on pricing. This is both the deepest pain and the least-contested territory — the moat.
_Proof: E-009, E-010, E-011, E-023. Patterns P3, P6. Insight I1._

**T3 — Buyers buy professional identity before they buy utility.**
The most emotionally charged buyer language isn't about time saved — it's about feeling like a real professional and being validated by gatekeepers. The product is an identity upgrade first, a tool second.
_Proof: E-001, E-002 ("affirmed my sense of myself as a professional artist"), E-003. Pattern P2._

**T4 — Incumbents are trusted on inventory but distrusted on billing, price hikes, and empathy.**
The leaders' worst customer-voice failures cluster around money and care: surprise charges, refused refunds, PE-driven price increases with no new value, reports that dump you back into Excel, "built by developers, not gallerists." Trust mechanics are a differentiation surface, not hygiene.
_Proof: E-015, E-016, E-017, E-018, E-022. Pattern P4._

**T5 — The growing market is the low/solo segment incumbents actively price out.**
Artlogic "only makes sense for established artists" (£492/yr); Artwork Archive competes on cheap inventory; market data shows the sub-$500k segment growing double digits while the $50k–$250k middle is down 29% since 2010. The abandoned segment and the growing segment are the same segment — and it is our ICP.
_Proof: E-019, E-023, E-026, E-027. Pattern P5. Insight I3._

**T6 — "All-in-one" is table stakes, already owned — not a differentiator.**
Consolidation is the most common praise for the incumbent, who owns "affordable, intuitive, all-in-one" with a 4.9★/106-review wall. "One app for your entire art business" can support, but cannot lead.
_Proof: E-001, E-023, E-024. Pattern P6._

**T7 — Transaction volume is decoupling from art value, favoring per-seat tooling on direct/social channels.**
Total value fell while transaction count rose; activity is migrating to lower price points and higher counts; online and social channels (Instagram/WeChat/DM) are structurally elevated post-2019. A per-artist-seat model is insulated from art-price swings and aligned with where selling actually happens.
_Proof: E-027, E-028, E-030. Pattern P7. Insight I3._

**T8 — The manager's distinguishing pain is paying a roster correctly; mainstream tools ignore it, so managers cobble workarounds.**
The manager ICP's stated unmet need is roster-wide commission/consignment accounting — per-artist splits, auto-calculated payouts, per-seat access. Mainstream POS ignores it (a 25–30-artist gallery resorts to consignment-shop software never built for art); the closest art-native competitor, ArtCloud (now Artlogic/PE-owned), has already built exactly this. So roster settlement is both the manager wedge ("pay every artist correctly, automatically") **and** a competitive table-stakes bar — match the workflow, win on pricing + ownership.
_Proof: E-031, E-033, E-034. Pattern P8. Insight I8._

**T9 — France already trusts a native deterministic pricing method that maps almost 1:1 onto Artelier's engine.**
"Le calcul au point" + "la cote d'artiste" is a standardized, gallery-taught, size-and-career pricing system — the same logic Artelier's engine runs, in the market's own vocabulary. French pricing anxiety is identical to anglophone (more emotionally framed); the formula objection is more explicit and must be pre-empted ("the calcul sets the floor, not the cage"). In France, Artelier should automate the method the market already uses, not teach a new one.
_Proof: E-035, E-036, E-037. Pattern P9. Insights I6, A8._

**T10 — A dated French legal forcing function is about to push the entire ICP off spreadsheets — but only onto compliant tooling.**
The French e-invoicing reform legally compels every small VAT-subject artist/manager to **receive** structured e-invoices from 09/2026 and **emit** them from 09/2027; an emailed PDF is explicitly disqualified. The "change-adoption" friction that is Artelier's core problem is being solved by the state, on Artelier's launch timeline — but the tailwind only applies to tools that emit compliant invoices through an approved platform (PDP), which Artelier currently does not.
_Proof: E-038, E-039. Pattern P10. Insight I7. Anti-pattern A7._

## What we commit to doing about them

- **We sell pricing, not organization.** Inventory is a red ocean owned by Artwork Archive; pricing is open water. Lead every funnel with it. _(T2; I1)_
- **We out-workflow, we do not out-feature.** The 7-stage inquiry→one-sheet→invoice→sell→provenance→ship sequence wins because the leader is "built by developers, not gallerists." _(T4; E-016; I4)_
- **We are the anti-extraction, founder-owned, own-your-data alternative.** Founder ownership and the 15% community fund are competitive positioning the evidence supports, not just ethics. _[The société-à-mission legal binding of the give-back is an open question — see decision-rights.]_ _(T4; E-008, E-017; I2)_
- **We instrument the channels artists already use; we do not build a marketplace.** Put a transaction backbone behind the DM. _(T7; E-030; I4)_
- **The trial is the pitch.** Value must land in days; a broken calculator or silent bulk-upload failure confirms the buyer's fear that switching is more pain than the spreadsheet. Bug-fixes are upstream of the entire funnel. _(E-013, E-014; I5)_
- **For managers, lead with settlement.** "Pay every artist correctly, automatically" is the manager headline; per-seat access and per-artist consignment are table stakes we build to match, not market as novel. _(T8; I8)_
- **In France, speak the market's language.** Use "calcul au point" and "cote d'artiste" natively; position the engine as a defensible floor, not a cage, to pre-empt the "trop mathématique / injuste" objection. _(T9; I6; A8)_
- **Treat the e-invoicing mandate as a wedge only if we're actually compliant.** Make the PDP/compliance decision before any "compliant French invoicing" claim ships. _(T10; I7; A7)_

## Three things competitors don't act like they believe _[synthesis from P2–P6]_

1. That **pricing**, not inventory, is the job to be done.
2. That **how a tool treats people around money** (cancellation, price hikes, refunds) is itself a product.
3. That the **low/solo segment** is the market to build for, not the one to graduate past.
