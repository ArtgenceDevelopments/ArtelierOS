# Research Brief — Artelier Business Evidence Pass

**Date:** 2026-06-11
**Brand:** Artelier Business — a business operating system for living, primary-market artists and the people who manage them. https://artelierbusiness.com
**Defensible core:** a deterministic, rules-based pricing engine. Internal framing: *"AI to do the typing, math to do the pricing."* Positioned as a "Business Companion," pitched as *"one app for your entire art business."*

## Core buyer archetypes (CORRECTED scope)
The brief as originally drafted listed "Artists, Artist managers, curators, foundations and residencies." That is the **old six-segment framing that has already been retired.** It is corrected here, because researching the wrong audience poisons every downstream pattern.

The locked ICP is a single spectrum:
1. **Solo artist (roster of one)** — sells directly, often via social/DM, drowning in spreadsheets, anxious about pricing.
2. **Small artist-manager (2–15 artists)** — Marques's own position; manages a roster, needs per-artist separation and a sale workflow.
3. **Growing professional manager (15+ artists)** — the upper end of the same value metric (per-artist-under-management seat).

**Explicitly NOT the ICP:** galleries, universities/curators, foundations, residencies, institutions. Evidence below shows *why* this narrowing is correct, not just asserted.

**What they share:** they handle artwork, photos from studios/events, and they all hit the same three walls — pricing, inventory sprawl, and the inquiry→sale→provenance→ship workflow that nobody tools well for them.

## Core problem
The friction that keeps a buyer from moving from interest to confidence is **change + technology adoption**: the incumbent workflow is a spreadsheet plus memory plus DMs, and switching feels like more work than the pain it removes — until the spreadsheet breaks under volume. The switch trigger is that breaking moment.

## Competitors / substitutes
- **Artwork Archive** — the beloved, cheap ($6/mo) inventory incumbent. 4.9★, 106 Capterra reviews. Owns "affordable + all-in-one inventory."
- **Artlogic** — the gallery standard (~6,000 galleries). PE-owned. Powerful, expensive (£492/yr+), "built by developers, not gallerists," billing-trust complaints.
- **ArtPlacer** — AR / "view in a room" marketing tool. Adjacent, not core overlap. Pricing-transparency complaints.
- **Canvy / ArtFlows / Valise (Airtable)** — visualization and DIY-database substitutes.
- **"Do nothing" / DIY spreadsheet** — the true default competitor and the one most evidence points back to.

## Hunting grounds
Capterra / G2 / Software Advice / Shopify App Store reviews; artist Substacks and blogs; Etsy seller forums; Art Basel & UBS report; independent review/comparison sites run by would-be competitors (a rich, underused source).

## Two honest method notes
1. **Verbatim length is capped.** Reviews and forum posts are copyrighted. Entries capture the *distinctive fragment* (short, verbatim) plus the live link to the full source, rather than reproducing whole reviews. The distinctive phrase is the data; the link carries the rest.
2. **No screenshots were fabricated.** Competitive entries are logged as `document` format with the live URL + capture date (2026-06-11). Dated screenshots for the visual archive should be captured manually on the next pass — that is a logged next-pass action, not a silent gap.
