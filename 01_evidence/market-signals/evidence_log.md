### Evidence #E-027
**Name:** Middle market ($50k–$250k works) down 29% since 2010
**Evidence Type:** market_signal
**Source Type:** Market Analysis
**Evidence Format:** metric
**Link:** https://www.artsy.net/article/artsy-editorial-global-art-market-rebounded-596-billion-2025-art-basel-ubs-report-finds
**Source Date:** 2026-03-17
**Theme Buckets:** risk | behavior
**Journey Phase:** aware
**Confidence:** high

> Sales of works priced $50,000–$250,000 are down 29% since 2010; volume migrating to lower price points and higher transaction counts.

**Reveals:** The squeezed middle is hollowing out; activity is moving to lower prices and more transactions. More transactions at lower values = more demand for cheap, fast, per-transaction tooling. That's a per-seat SaaS tailwind. Quantify this shift in the raise — it's the structural "why now."

### Evidence #E-028
**Name:** 40.5M transactions, +3% volume even as value fell
**Evidence Type:** market_signal
**Source Type:** Market Analysis
**Evidence Format:** metric
**Link:** https://theartmarket.artbasel.com/the-art-market-2025/global-market
**Source Date:** 2025-04-08
**Theme Buckets:** behavior
**Journey Phase:** aware
**Confidence:** high

> Transaction count grew 3% to 40.5 million in 2024 even as total value fell 12% — value and volume are decoupling.

**Reveals:** Artelier monetizes per-artist / per-workflow, not per-dollar-of-art — so it benefits from rising *transaction volume* regardless of art prices. This insulates the model from art-market value swings. Say exactly this to any investor worried about buying into a "down" art market: the rail charges per transaction, and transactions are up.
